Earrings

    As in every cultural development, earrings were first encountered by the Egyptians. Gold rings, which were very large in the past, are replaced by smaller pendant-shaped earrings over time. In Babylonians and later Asian civilizations, the earring is a jewelry that only men wear and that indicates rank in society. As the art of gold embroidery evolves, it becomes more elegant. While the Greeks used gold earrings with rattles, the Romans put precious stones on the earrings. Earrings, which were not used much in the Middle Ages when freedoms were limited, became fashionable again with the Renaissance. While women wear the earrings in both ears, men only wear them in their left ears. Earrings, which have become an integral part of jewelry today, are presented to us by combining them with precious stones. The earrings decorated with precious stones such as diamonds, sapphires, rubies and emeralds are also designed to appeal to all tastes by being decorated with affordable stones such as zircon in order to be suit.

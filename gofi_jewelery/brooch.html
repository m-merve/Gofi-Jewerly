Brooches

    It is said that in ancient Egypt, kings especially used talismanic brooches. For this reason, brooches are believed to have a magical power. The real discoverers of the power of jewelry were the Uyghur Turks and then the Seljuks. The Ottoman civilization, on the other hand, has fascinated itself throughout history by symbolizing its magnificence and power with jewellery. Jewelry and blanks, which are symbols of social status, did not lose their influence even when modernization began in the 19th century in the Ottoman Empire. It has even been accepted as the hallmark of nobility. Brooches, one of the most precious jewels surviving today, are no longer a status indicator. In the modern world, it is preferred because it is the most obvious way of expressing oneself. Because brooch can be used in the most striking points of clothes or accessories. For the same reason, a sultan without a brooch is rarely depicted in the Ottoman Empire. What century does your soul belong to, or if you want to express yourself, try using a brooch. Optionally, the material of the brooch is selected and if there is a stone in the design, it is added from the options of diamond, emerald or ruby. Depending on the options, the price of the brooch also varies.
